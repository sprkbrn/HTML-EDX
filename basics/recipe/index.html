Maple Salmon

This was one of the first dishes I learned to make. It was both simple and rewarding reinforcing my confidence in my ability to cook a good meal.

I highly suggest you create this dish with boiled asparagus. When made just right, the salmon practically dissolves in your mouth as a profile of sweetness against the bitterness of the asparagus.



This is one easy but fun dish to make and can be enjoyed any time of the year. I personally never expected to enjoy the blend of maple and mustard but, somehow they work just right.

How to prepare:

  1. Thaw salmon, heat oven to 325 degrees.

  2. Slice cilantro crosswise, seperating steps from the leaves.

  3. Transfer the stems into a bowl, stir in maple syrup, mustard and mayonnaise until well mixed.

  4. Season salmon with salt & pepper, place in baking dish, slather the maple syrup mixture generously over the salmon.

  5. Bake for 15 - 20 minutes or until salmon is easy to slice. The blade should be warm to the touch after slicing to the center.

  6. Top with cilantro leaves and squeezed lemon just before serving.

Ingredient List:

To make this recipe you're going to need:

  1. 1/2 lb of skinless salmon (you can also just remove the skin yourself)

  2. 12 or so fresh cilantro sprigs

  3. 2 tbsp pure maple syrup

  4. 2 tbsp dijon mustard

  5. 1 tbsp mayonnaise

  6. Kosher salt

  7. Ground black pepper

  8. Lemon wedges (for serving)
